## What To Do in a Plumbing Emergency in Porto

### Immediate Steps (Before Calling Anyone)

**Stay calm.** Most plumbing emergencies look worse than they are.

**Step 1: Turn Off the Main Water Supply**

This is the most critical action. Stopping the water flow stops the damage.

The main water valve (válvula de corte or válvula de shut-off) is usually located:
- **In apartments:** Inside the apartment, often in a closet, under the kitchen sink, or in a corner bathroom
- **In houses:** Outside near the front, or in a basement/utility room
- **In Porto's older buildings:** Sometimes in a cellar or shared utility space

**How to identify it:** Look for a small metal lever or knob. It's typically brass or chrome, 1-2 inches in diameter.

**How to turn it off:**
- If it's a **lever:** Turn it perpendicular (90 degrees) to the pipe direction. The lever should be parallel to the wall.
- If it's a **knob or handle:** Turn clockwise (right) until it stops. Don't force it — stop when resistance is felt.

**After turning off the main valve:**
- Open all your faucets (kitchen, bathroom, outdoor hose) to release residual water pressure
- This prevents additional water from being trapped in pipes, which can cause further damage

**Step 2: Locate the Problem Source**

If possible, identify where the leak is coming from:
- **Burst pipe:** Water spraying from a visible pipe
- **Leak from under sink or toilet:** Water pooling beneath fixtures
- **Water dripping from ceiling:** Leak from apartment above (alert your upstairs neighbor if it's theirs)
- **Backed-up toilet or drain:** Water backing up into tub, shower, or multiple drains

Document the problem with a photo (for your insurance and for the contractor).

**Step 3: Stop the Immediate Damage**

- **Spread towels** to contain water and prevent it from seeping into floors/ceilings
- **Move furniture** or electronics away from water
- **Don't use electrical appliances** in or near the water (electrical hazard)
- **Unplug refrigerators or other devices** if water is nearby

## What To Do in an Electrical Emergency

### Immediate Steps (Before Calling Anyone)

**Electrical emergencies are dangerous.** Your safety comes first.

**Step 1: Identify the Problem**

- **No power in one room?** Circuit breaker may have tripped
- **No power in multiple rooms or entire apartment?** Main breaker may have tripped or there's a fault
- **Sparking or burning smell?** Possible electrical fire — evacuate immediately, call firefighters (112)
- **Water and electricity together?** Evacuate immediately — electrocution risk

**Step 2: Check the Circuit Breaker Panel**

If you have partial power loss (one room dark, others working), check the circuit breaker (disjuntor):

**Location:** Usually in your apartment entrance, a closet, or utility room. It's a rectangular gray or white box on the wall.

**How to identify a tripped breaker:**
- Open the panel (usually by unlatching a small door)
- Look for a switch that's in the middle position or different from others
- The tripped switch will look "off" or halfway between "on" and "off"

**How to reset it:**
- Push the tripped switch fully to the "off" position (down)
- Then push it to the "on" position (up)
- Wait 5 seconds
- If power returns, the breaker has reset

**If the breaker trips again immediately:** Don't reset it again. There's a fault. Call an emergency electrician.

**Step 3: Turn Off Power to the Problem Area**

If you can identify which circuit is faulty:
- Switch that circuit's breaker to "off"
- This isolates the problem and prevents danger

**Main breaker:** If you can't identify a single faulty circuit, you can turn off the main breaker (disjuntor geral) to cut all power to the apartment. It's usually the largest switch in the panel, at the top.

**Do NOT attempt electrical repair yourself** if you're not qualified. Electricity in Portugal is 230V — serious hazard.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I fix a plumbing leak myself?
Small leaks (dripping faucet, loose connection) can be tightened by you. Major leaks (burst pipe, active flooding) require a professional. If you're not confident, call a plumber. The cost (€100-300) is cheaper than water damage to your apartment and your neighbor's.

### How do I know if I have an electrical problem vs. just a tripped breaker?
If one room loses power and you can reset a tripped breaker, it's likely a temporary overload (you plugged in too many devices). If the breaker trips again immediately, or if multiple areas lose power, call an electrician. If you see sparks or smell burning, call immediately.

### Is 24-hour service available in Porto?
Yes. Emergency plumbers and electricians work 24/7. Night/weekend service costs 50-100% more than daytime rates, but it's available when you need it.

### What if I can't describe the problem in Portuguese?
Vitfix's app supports English. When you request an emergency contractor, specify that you speak English. Many contractors in Porto work with expats and speak enough English to understand emergency problems. Photos and simple gestures help communication.

### Can I claim emergency service costs on my apartment insurance?
Often yes. Document the problem with photos, keep the contractor's invoice (fatura), and report to your insurance company. Many policies cover emergency repair costs for burst pipes, electrical faults, etc.

### What's the difference between a plumber (canalizador) and a plumbing engineer?
A **canalizador** handles repairs and replacements (leaks, fixtures, drain cleaning). An **engenheiro de plumbagem** (plumbing engineer) designs and oversees complex plumbing systems (new installations, major renovations). For emergencies, you need a canalizador.

### Should I hire the cheapest emergency contractor?

### What should I do if the problem recurs after the contractor leaves?
Contact the contractor immediately. Most offer a 24-48 hour warranty on their work. If the same issue recurs, they should return and fix it at no additional cost. This is why verifying contractor credentials matters — insured professionals stand behind their work.

### Can I negotiate emergency service costs?
Difficult. Emergency service is premium service — availability, speed, and after-hours work justify higher costs. You can compare quotes from multiple contractors via Vitfix and choose the best value (considering response time and reviews), but prices are fairly standardized.

### What if I don't have money right now to pay for emergency service?

## Common Emergency Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Ignoring "Minor" Water Leaks
A small drip seems harmless — until it causes hidden mold or structural damage. Fix even small leaks quickly. Cost now (€100-200) saves thousands in damage later.

### Mistake 2: Using Electrical Appliances Near Water
Never plug in a hairdryer, phone charger, or other device if water is nearby. Electrocution risk is real. Turn off power to wet areas and call an electrician.

### Mistake 3: Calling a Non-Verified Contractor
In emergencies, you're desperate. An unverified contractor may charge triple the standard rate ("emergency surcharge"), perform shoddy work, or disappear without proper invoice/warranty. Use Vitfix to find verified professionals, even in emergencies.

### Mistake 4: Not Turning Off the Main Water Valve
Waiting for the plumber while water floods your apartment costs thousands in damage. Taking 2 minutes to turn off the main valve stops the damage immediately. Do this before calling anyone.

### Mistake 5: Panicking and Overpaying
In emergencies, you're stressed and not thinking clearly. A professional may sense this and quote high. Get a quote, ask about response time and pricing, and decide calmly. If you feel pressured, request a different contractor.
